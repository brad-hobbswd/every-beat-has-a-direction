Afterword

One Final Word

Before you close this book, allow me to speak to you one last time, not simply as an author, but as a fellow follower of Jesus Christ.

If you have read this far, thank you. Of all the books you could have chosen, you invested your time in these pages, and I do not take that lightly. My prayer has been the same from the moment I began writing. I have not wanted to win an argument. I have wanted to point people toward Jesus Christ.

This book was never written to tell you what songs you may or may not place on your playlist. It was written because I believe the condition of the heart matters deeply to God. Music is only one of many influences shaping our lives, but because it speaks so powerfully to our emotions, memories, and affections, it deserves thoughtful examination through the light of Scripture.

As you leave these pages, I hope you do not walk away thinking primarily about genres, artists, or musical preferences. I hope you walk away thinking about Jesus. If your love for Him has grown deeper, if your desire to know His Word has increased, if your prayer life has become richer, or if you have simply become more intentional about guarding your heart, then this book has accomplished far more than I could have hoped.

"The greatest soundtrack you will ever have is a life that faithfully follows Jesus Christ."

The world will never stop singing. Every generation will produce new voices, new trends, and new messages competing for your attention. Some will disappear as quickly as they arrive. Others will leave lasting influence upon culture. Yet through every generation, one voice remains unchanged. The voice of Jesus Christ still calls ordinary people to deny themselves, take up their cross, and follow Him. His invitation has never changed, and neither has His promise to remain with those who belong to Him.

You will not remember every sentence you have read in this book, and that is perfectly all right. If you remember only one thing, remember this. Guard your heart because it belongs to Jesus Christ. Fill your mind with His Word. Listen carefully for the voice of the Good Shepherd. Worship Him sincerely. Love Him wholeheartedly. Follow Him faithfully. When your heart belongs completely to Him, many of life's decisions become wonderfully clear because your greatest desire has already been settled.

One day, every earthly song will come to an end. Every concert will end. Every playlist will eventually fall silent. One day, faith will become sight, and those who belong to Christ will stand before the One whose voice first called them into His marvelous light. On that day, nothing else will matter more than knowing Him.

Until then, keep walking. Keep listening. Keep worshiping. Keep growing. Keep your eyes upon Jesus.

May every beat of your life lead you closer to Jesus Christ until faith becomes sight and worship never ends.

Grace and peace,

Bradley Hobbs

Before you close this book, allow me to leave you with one final encouragement. Continue pursuing Jesus Christ with all your heart. There will always be another opinion to consider, another trend to follow, another voice competing for your attention, and another song asking for a place within your heart. Yet there is only one Savior who laid down His life for you, only one Shepherd who faithfully leads His sheep, and only one King whose Kingdom will never end.

My prayer is not that you would leave these pages with greater fear of the world but with greater love for Jesus Christ. When He becomes the greatest affection of your heart, discernment becomes less about asking, "What am I allowed to do?" and more about asking, "What will help me know Him more deeply?" That question has the power to shape every decision you make for the rest of your life.

Perhaps our paths will never cross on this side of eternity. Yet if we both remain faithful to Jesus Christ, we will one day stand together before His throne with countless believers from every nation, tribe, people, and language. On that day, every earthly song will have fulfilled its purpose, and together we will join the everlasting worship that never grows silent, never grows weary, and never comes to an end.

"Until that day, let every beat of your life echo the glory of the King."

Thank you for allowing me to walk beside you through these pages. More importantly, thank you for giving Jesus Christ your attention. If this book has encouraged you to open your Bible more often, spend more time in prayer, worship with greater sincerity, or examine your heart more honestly before God, then every hour spent writing these pages has been worthwhile.

May the Lord bless you and keep you. May He establish your steps. May His Word dwell richly within you. May your worship remain sincere. May your heart remain tender. May your ears remain attentive to the voice of the Good Shepherd. And may every beat of your life lead others to Jesus Christ.

Grace and peace,

Bradley Hobbs

"The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen."

Revelation 22:21 (AMPC)
